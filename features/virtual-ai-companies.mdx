## What Are Virtual AI Companies?

Virtual AI Companies are simulated tech startups where you work as a real team member. Instead of just completing exercises, you get assigned to a themed company, receive a job title based on your career track, and work on engineering tickets — the same way you would at an actual AI company.

This is the feature that separates Safua from every other learning platform. You don't just learn skills. You practice being an engineer.

## How It Works

### 1. Company Assignment

When you reach **Track Level 2**, you're automatically assigned to one of our themed AI companies:

| Company | Industry | What They Build |
|---------|----------|-----------------|
| **NovaMind AI** | Enterprise AI | Conversational AI assistants for workflows |
| **DataForge Labs** | Data Infrastructure | Streaming ETL pipelines for ML teams |
| **VisionArc** | Computer Vision | Automated defect detection for manufacturing |
| **Sentient Health** | Healthcare AI | Clinical decision support systems |

Each company has a unique tech stack, product focus, and AI team lead persona. You're placed in the company with the fewest members for balanced teams.

### 2. Your Role

Your role is determined by your career track:

| Career Track | Role |
|-------------|------|
| AI Engineer | Junior AI Engineer |
| ML Engineer | Junior ML Engineer |
| Data Scientist | Junior Data Scientist |
| Data Engineer | Junior Data Engineer |
| Foundation | AI Engineering Intern |

### 3. Ticket System

Once assigned, you work on engineering tickets — realistic tasks generated by AI based on your company's product and your current skill level.

**Ticket flow (Kanban board):**

```
Backlog → In Progress → In Review → Done
```

Tickets include:
- **Title** — like a real Jira ticket (e.g., "Add retry logic to embedding pipeline")
- **Description** — 2-3 sentences explaining the task
- **Acceptance criteria** — what "done" looks like
- **Priority** — Low / Medium / High
- **Story points** — 2 (easy), 3 (medium), or 5 (hard)
- **Skills** — what you'll practice (e.g., Python, SQL, Testing)

You can generate new tickets anytime. The AI creates tasks matched to your track, level, and company context.

### 4. Daily Standup

Every day, you submit a standup update:

- **Yesterday** — what you worked on
- **Today** — what you plan to do
- **Blockers** — anything stopping you

Your AI team lead responds with personalized feedback — acknowledging your progress, suggesting priorities, and helping with blockers. Each team lead has a distinct personality and communication style.

### 5. Code Review

When you submit code for a ticket, an AI Senior Engineer reviews it. The review includes:

- **Overall status** — Approved, Changes Requested, or Needs Rework
- **Score** — 0-100 quality rating
- **Inline comments** — specific feedback on code lines
- **Overall feedback** — constructive, specific, actionable
- **"What a senior would do differently"** — architecture, patterns, and performance insights a more experienced engineer would apply

If your code is approved, the ticket moves to Done automatically.

| Action | XP |
|--------|----|

| Code review approved | **+25 XP bonus** |

## Why This Matters

Most learning platforms teach you skills in isolation. You complete exercises, get a certificate, and still feel unprepared for a real job.

Virtual AI Companies close that gap. After working through tickets, standups, and code reviews, you have:

1. **Experience with real workflows** — Kanban boards, ticket management, code review processes
2. **Practice communicating** — writing standups, documenting code, responding to review feedback
3. **Senior-level feedback** — understanding what production-quality code actually looks like
4. **Proof of work** — completed tickets and reviewed code for your portfolio

This is the closest thing to on-the-job training without having the job yet.
