## What Is a Cohort?

A cohort is a group of 4–8 learners matched by the platform. Your cohort peers are:
- On the **same career track** as you
- At a **similar overall progress level** (within ~20%)
- Strong in **different skills** than you — so you can learn from each other

Cohorts are designed to replicate the experience of working on a real team: accountability, peer feedback, healthy competition, and the kind of learning that only happens when you have to explain something to someone else.

## How Matching Works

The matching algorithm runs every **Sunday at midnight UTC**. It:

1. Finds all learners who aren't yet in an active cohort
2. Builds a **skill mastery vector** for each learner from their Living Learning Graph
3. Scores every possible pair: 40% weight on similar progress level, 60% weight on complementary skills
4. Groups learners into cohorts of 4–8 using those scores

You'll receive a notification when you've been matched.

## What Your Cohort Does

### Weekly Challenges
Every Monday, your cohort gets a new coding challenge related to your track. Submit your solution by Sunday. Examples:
- AI Engineer: "Build a Mini RAG Pipeline"
- Data Scientist: "Model Bake-Off — best F1 score wins"
- ML Engineer: "Optimise a slow PyTorch training loop by 3x"
- Data Engineer: "Build a dbt project with staging, intermediate, and mart layers"

**+150 XP** for submitting. Bonus XP for the highest score.

### Monthly Competitions
Once a month, your cohort runs a bigger competition — a capstone-style challenge judged by your peers. **+500 XP for the winner.**

### Peer Code Reviews
When you submit a lab, you're automatically assigned a cohort peer to review it. You have **48 hours** to give feedback. Your reviewer gets **+50 XP** for completing the review.

A good review includes:
- What they did well (specific)
- What could be improved (specific, with suggestion)
- A score out of 100

### Standup Feed
A simple async feed where cohort members post daily updates. Even one sentence ("Got through the RAG module, struggled with re-ranking") keeps the cohort active and earns you streak credit.

### Weekly Leaderboard
A weekly XP ranking within your cohort. Resets every Monday. No prizes — just bragging rights and the motivation to keep going.

## Cohort Health Score

Every cohort has a **health score** from 0–100, updated weekly:

| Component | Weight | What it measures |
|---|---|---|
| Standup rate | 30% | % of members who posted a standup this week |
| Peer review completion | 40% | % of reviews completed on time |
| Challenge submission rate | 20% | % of members who submitted the weekly challenge |
| Active member rate | 10% | % of members with any activity |

A **healthy cohort** (score ≥ 70) is one where everyone shows up. If your cohort health drops below 40, admins will check in.

## Tips

- **Review quickly.** Your reviewer is waiting. 48 hours feels like plenty but goes fast.
- **Be specific in your feedback.** "Looks good" doesn't help anyone.
- **Post standups even on hard days.** "Stuck on the async stuff, coming back tomorrow" is a valid standup.
- **Use the leaderboard as motivation, not comparison.** Everyone in your cohort is at roughly the same level.
