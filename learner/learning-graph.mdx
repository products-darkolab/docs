## What Is the Living Learning Graph?

The Living Learning Graph is a directed graph of 210+ skills, where edges represent prerequisites. Every learner has their own **mastery score** for each skill — a number between 0 and 1 that reflects how well they know it.

Unlike a simple "lesson completed" checkbox, mastery scores:
- **Increase** when you complete labs and answer questions correctly
- **Decay** over time if you don't revisit them
- **Drive recommendations** for what to learn next

## Mastery Scores

| Score Range | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 0.0 – 0.3 | Not yet learned or largely forgotten |
| 0.3 – 0.6 | Developing — knows the basics, needs reinforcement |
| 0.6 – 0.85 | Solid understanding |
| 0.85 – 1.0 | Expert — ready to teach this skill to others |

### How mastery updates

When you complete a lab or review, your mastery score updates using a weighted blend that rewards consistency over cramming:

```
new_mastery = (old_mastery × 0.7) + (performance_score × 0.3)
```

A single perfect lab score won't shoot you to 1.0 — you build mastery through repeated, spaced exposure.

### Daily decay

Skills decay at 2% per day if not reviewed. This mirrors the well-documented **forgetting curve** — knowledge fades without reinforcement. The decay system:
- Keeps your graph honest (no false expertise from a lesson you did 6 months ago)
- Triggers spaced repetition reviews before important skills fall below a threshold
- Makes the ZPD recommendations more accurate over time

## Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD)

The system recommends the **top 5 skills** where you're in your ZPD — the zone where you know enough to learn, but haven't mastered it yet.

A skill is in your ZPD if:
1. Your mastery is between **0.4 and 0.75** (developing, not expert)
2. All its **prerequisites are met** (mastery ≥ 0.6)

This means the graph never recommends something you're not ready for, and never wastes your time on something you already know.

## The Skill Graph Visualisation

On your `/learning-graph` page, you'll see an interactive D3.js force-directed graph:

- **Node size** = your mastery score (bigger = higher mastery)
- **Node colour** = track (blue = AI Engineer, green = Data Scientist, etc.)
- **Edge** = prerequisite relationship
- **Hover** = skill name, mastery score, last reviewed date
- **Click** = opens the relevant module

You can zoom, pan, and drag nodes. Use it to see your progress visually and identify skills that have decayed (small nodes) or gaps in your prerequisite chain.
